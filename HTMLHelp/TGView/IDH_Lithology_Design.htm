Lithology Design

 

 

The Design tab sheet in the Lithology Dialog Box contains the controls for designing a lithology column.

 

A lithology column consists of lithologic units, which are a sections of sediment with top depths and bottom depths. Lithologic units are composed of lithologic components. Some examples of lithologic components are, in Troels-Smith terminology, Limus detrituosus (gyttja), Limus calcareus (marl), and Argilla steatodes (clay).

 

The depths of lithologic units are entered in the worksheet in the upper right-hand corner of the dialog box. A sample of the pattern for the unit is shown in the upper left-hand side. Components of the lithologic unit are selected in the tab panel at the bottom of the tab sheet.
