For Type of Analysis, choose Stratigraphically constrained or Unconstrained. Stratigraphically constrained is normally selected for stratigraphic data. This method forces the analysis to consider only stratigraphically adjacent samples or clusters during the agglomerative process. Consequently, at any stage in the analysis, every cluster is comprised of a set of stratigraphically contiguous samples. Unconstrained is typically chosen for surface samples that have no prior stratigraphic order. However, an unconstrained analysis of stratigraphic data can be useful for identifying recurrence of pollen assemblages in time. See Hansen et al. (2001) for an example of this kind of analysis.
