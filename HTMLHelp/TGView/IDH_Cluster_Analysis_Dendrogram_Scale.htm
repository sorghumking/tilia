CONISS Dendrogram Scales

Various criteria can be used to define the heights of nodes connecting clusters in a CONISS cluster analysis, and all of these appear in the literature. Each of these scales provides different information about the analysis. Dendrograms scaled by either Increase in sum of squares or Mean within-cluster sum of squares are subject to reversals. Dendrograms scaled by either Within-cluster sum of squares or Mean within-cluster sum of squares provide information about individual clusters. The former is highly dependent on cluster size. Total sum of squares (the default) is not subject to reversals and illustrates the progressive formation of the clusters—the node of each merger is above the nodes of all previous mergers. In general, Total sum of squares and Mean within-cluster sum of squares provide the most useful information.
