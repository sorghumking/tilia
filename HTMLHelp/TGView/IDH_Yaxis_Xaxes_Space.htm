Distance Between Ends of Y-axis and Data

 

 

The distance between the ends of the Y-axis and the data is shown by the longer red arrow to the left of the graph above. The distance is in virtual units. The default value is 0.3. The X-axes are always plotted 0.2 units from the ends of the Y-axis, so by default the distance between the X-axes and the data is 0.3 - 0.2 = 0.1.

 

For a histogram style graph, the distance between the ends of the Y-axis and the data is from the top (or bottom) of the Y-axis to the middle of the top (or bottom) histogram bar. Increasing the distance will prevent wide bars from overlapping the X-axes.

 

 

 

Tip:

  • You can also increase the distance between the ends of the Y-axes and the data by changing the minimum and maximum Y-axis Limits.
