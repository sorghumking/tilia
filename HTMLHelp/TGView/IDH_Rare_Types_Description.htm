Rare Types

Sometimes rare occurrences of a variable are important to show, but the percentages are so small that they are barely visible on the diagram. In this case, symbols can show the stratigraphic occurrence each individual counted. Rare types are designated in the original spreadsheet. They are used for percentage calculations of other variables, but they are not converted to percentages themselves. For rare types to be plotted, the graph style must be silhouette or histogram. Below is an example from Crawford Lake, Ontario that shows an episode of cereal grasses before European settlement, which is marked by Ambrosia-type pollen.
