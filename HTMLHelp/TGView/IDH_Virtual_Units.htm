In contrast to many graphics programs in which a chart or diagram is constructed in actual units (e.g. cm or inches), the diagram in Tilia is constructed in virtual units, which are arbitrary units. The final diagram is then projected to the fit the display or paper size of the printer. Consequently, the diagram may be designed and printed to different paper sizes without having to explicitly re-scale the diagram. Virtual units may be thought of as the arbitrary units on a large piece of graph paper on which diagram is drawn. With respect to font sizes, the units on this graph paper may be thought of as inches, with the default Y-axis 10 inches high. See notes below.

 

Notes

  • The default diagram has a Y-axis 10 units high.

  • Although you select font sizes in points in the font dialog boxes, these are virtual units. A font size of 72 points = 1 virtual unit.

  • Line widths are measured in actual widths (points).
